Hi! My name is Pablo Martínez-Miravé and I am a Postdoctoral Fellow on Theoretical Particle Astrophysics. Currently, I work at Niels Bohr Institute, University of Copenhagen (Copenhagen, Denmark), as part of the AstroNU group .

Regarding my background, I studied a BSc in Physics and a master in Advanced Physics (in the path of Theoretical Physics) at University of Valencia. In automn 2018, I started my PhD studies with a FPU fellowship.

During my PhD, my research was mainly focused on the study of neutrino properties in terrestrial laboratories and from cosmology, both within and Beyond the Standard Model.

At present, my work focuses on particle astrophysics. I am interested in exploring standard and non-standard neutrino properties relying on terrestrial experiments, the Sun, the early Universe, as well as core-collapse supernovae and binary neutron star mergers. I also explore neutrino emission in astrophysical sources from thermal and thermonuclear processes, typically in the keV-MeV energy region.

Currently, my work at the Niels Bohr Institute is currently supported by the Villum Foundation (Project No. 13164).
